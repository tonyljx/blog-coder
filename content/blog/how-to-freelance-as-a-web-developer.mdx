Freelancing looks glamorous from the outside and feels like cold-emailing from the inside, at least at first.

The work is real. Finding the work is the actual skill.

## Where first clients come from

- People who already know you. Tell them what you are doing.
- Small businesses with visibly broken websites.
- Communities where you have been genuinely helpful first.

Charge for outcomes, not hours, as soon as you can describe the outcome. And write everything down: a one-page scope saves a dozen awkward conversations later.
